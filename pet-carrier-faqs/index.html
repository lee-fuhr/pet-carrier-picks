Pet Carrier questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Choosing the right pet carrier means matching the style to your travel mode, your pet’s size and temperament, and airline rules if you fly. Prioritise a well-ventilated, secure design that lets your pet stand, turn, and lie down comfortably, not just the cheapest option.

What type of pet carrier is best for air travel?

For air travel, you need a carrier that meets the airline’s specific under-seat dimensions, and those vary widely. In general, soft-sided carriers are easier to fit into tight spaces and often pass inspection more readily than hard-sided ones because they can squish down slightly. Look for a carrier with mesh panels on at least three sides for ventilation, a leak-proof bottom, and a secure zipper system. Many airlines also require that the carrier have a padded shoulder strap or carrying handle, and that your pet can stand and turn around inside. Always check your airline’s policy before buying, because some require hard-sided kennels for cargo hold travel on larger planes.

How do I measure my pet for a carrier?

Measure your pet from the tip of the nose to the base of the tail (length), from the floor to the top of the head when standing (height), and across the widest part of their body (width). Then add a few inches in each direction so your pet can move a little. The carrier length should be at least as long as your pet’s body, and the height should be high enough that your pet can stand without their head touching the top. Many carriers list internal dimensions, compare those to your pet’s measurements, not the external size. If your pet is between sizes, always go larger if airline rules allow.

What safety features should I look for in a pet carrier?

A secure latching or zippered closure is essential, look for zippers that can be locked with a small padlock or that have a self-locking slider. Some carriers come with a tether inside that clips to your pet’s collar or harness to prevent escape when you open the door. Ventilation is a safety feature too: panels should be sturdy mesh that won’t collapse, and there should be airflow on multiple sides. For hard-sided carriers, check that the door grille is rounded with no sharp edges. Also consider a carrier with a reflective trim or ID pocket so your contact info stays attached.

Are soft-sided or hard-sided carriers better?

Soft-sided carriers are lighter, more flexible for storage, and often more comfortable for your pet to lean against. They tend to have more mesh panels for airflow and are less intimidating for anxious pets. The downside: they offer less crash protection in a vehicle and can be crushed if stacked in cargo. Hard-sided carriers are sturdier, easier to clean, and generally meet airline cargo requirements. They also hold their shape better, so your pet won’t be squeezed. However, they’re heavier and take up more space. For car travel, a hard-sided carrier that you can buckle in with a seatbelt is the safer choice. For everyday vet trips, many owners prefer soft-sided for convenience.

Can I use a pet carrier for car travel?

Yes, but not all carriers are safe in a crash. A soft-sided carrier that isn’t secured can become a projectile. For car travel, choose a carrier that can be strapped in using a seatbelt, many have seatbelt loops or anchor points. Hard-sided carriers with a seatbelt pass-through are more protective. Never put a carrier in the front seat if there’s an airbag. Also make sure the carrier has good ventilation and that your pet can’t push a soft door open. If you drive often, consider a carrier that is crash-tested (though such models cost more). For short trips, a well-secured soft carrier may be fine, but for regular highway driving, invest in a sturdier option.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get my pet used to a carrier?

Place the carrier in a common area with the door open and a comfy blanket inside. Toss treats or toys in there so your pet explores willingly. Gradually increase the time the door is closed, starting with a few seconds and rewarding calm behaviour. Never force them in, that creates fear.

What if my pet is too big for an airline-approved under-seat carrier?

You’ll likely need to buy a larger hard-sided carrier that fits in the cargo hold. Check the airline’s maximum dimensions and weight for cargo travel. Unfortunately, large breeds often can’t fly in the cabin, so booking a pet-friendly airline with cargo options is necessary.

Are backpack pet carriers safe?

Backpack carriers are fine for calm, small pets on short walks, but they are not crash-rated for cars or suitable for flights. Look for one with ventilation openings and a padded interior. Make sure your pet can stand and turn, and never hang the backpack from a shoulder-strap only, always use both straps.

How do I clean a pet carrier?

Most soft-sided carriers have removable, machine-washable pads. For the carrier itself, spot-clean with mild soap and water, or wipe down with a pet-safe disinfectant. Hard-sided carriers can be hosed off and scrubbed with a brush. Always dry thoroughly before storing to prevent odours and mould.

Do I need a carrier for a small dog?

Yes, even small dogs benefit from a carrier during travel. It keeps them secure, reduces anxiety, and prevents them from jumping out or getting underfoot. For airline or public transport, a carrier is often required. For car rides, a crash-tested carrier is strongly recommended for any size dog.

Can I use a dog carrier for a cat?

Absolutely, as long as the carrier is appropriately sized and well-ventilated. Cats often prefer enclosed carriers that feel like a den, look for one with a top-opening door so you can lift them out easily. Ensure the carrier has no large gaps where a cat could slip through or get a paw stuck.

In shortThe right pet carrier balances comfort, safety, and practicality for how you actually travel. Focus on getting a solid fit, decent airflow, and a secure closure, everything else is a bonus. If you fly, check the airline specs first; if you drive, prioritise crash security. A good carrier makes every trip easier for both of you.
